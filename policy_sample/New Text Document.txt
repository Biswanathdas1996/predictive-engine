# **Mid-Year Salary Revision Policy**

*(Confidential – Internal Use Only)*

## **1. Purpose**

The purpose of this policy is to define a structured, transparent, and fair framework for granting **mid-year salary revisions** outside the annual compensation cycle. This ensures recognition of exceptional contributions, market alignment, and retention of critical talent.

---

## **2. Scope**

This policy applies to:

* All full-time employees of the organization
* Employees who have completed a minimum of **6 months of service**
* All business units and departments across global locations

---

## **3. Eligibility Criteria**

Employees may be considered for a mid-year salary revision under the following conditions:

### **3.1 Performance-Based**

* Consistently **exceeds performance expectations**
* Demonstrates measurable impact on business outcomes
* Recognized through internal performance frameworks (ratings, KPIs)

### **3.2 Role Enhancement / Promotion**

* Significant increase in responsibilities
* Internal role change or promotion outside the annual cycle

### **3.3 Market Correction**

* Salary significantly below market benchmarks
* Identified through compensation benchmarking exercises

### **3.4 Retention Cases**

* High-risk attrition cases
* Critical skill sets or key contributors identified by leadership

---

## **4. Policy Guidelines**

### **4.1 Frequency**

* Mid-year salary revisions are **exception-based**, not guaranteed
* Typically conducted **once between annual cycles**

### **4.2 Budget Allocation**

* Subject to **approved compensation budget**
* Allocated at organizational or business unit level

### **4.3 Increase Range**

* Typically ranges between **5% – 20% of base salary**
* Exceptions may require executive approval

### **4.4 Effective Date**

* Effective from the **approval date or predefined cycle date**
* No retrospective adjustments unless explicitly approved

---

## **5. Approval Process**

### **Step 1: Nomination**

* Initiated by **Reporting Manager**
* Supported with documented justification (performance, impact, market data)

### **Step 2: Business Review**

* Reviewed by **Business Unit Head**
* Validation of business need and budget availability

### **Step 3: HR Evaluation**

* Compensation benchmarking
* Internal parity check
* Policy compliance validation

### **Step 4: Final Approval**

* Approved by:

  * HR Leadership
  * Finance (if required)
  * Executive Leadership (for exceptions)

---

## **6. Documentation Requirements**

Each request must include:

* Employee details (role, tenure, current compensation)
* Justification note (business impact, achievements)
* Performance ratings (last review cycle)
* Market comparison (if applicable)
* Retention risk assessment (if applicable)

---

## **7. Communication**

* Approved revisions will be communicated formally via:

  * HR official communication (email/letter)
  * Updated compensation structure in HR systems
* Managers are responsible for **contextual discussion with employees**

---

## **8. Exclusions**

The following are **not eligible** for mid-year revisions:

* Employees under Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
* Employees serving notice period
* Contractual or temporary employees (unless specified)

---

## **9. Governance & Compliance**

* All decisions must adhere to:

  * Internal compensation policies
  * Equal pay and non-discrimination principles
* HR will conduct periodic audits to ensure fairness and consistency

---

## **10. Exceptions**

Any deviations from this policy require:

* Strong business justification
* Approval from **CHRO / Executive Leadership**

---

## **11. Review & Amendments**

This policy will be reviewed annually or as required based on:

* Market trends
* Business strategy
* Organizational needs

---

## **12. Key Principles**

* Meritocracy
* Transparency
* Market competitiveness
* Internal equity
* Retention of critical talent
